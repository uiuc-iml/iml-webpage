METHOD - Vocabulary

The following are terms used in the documentation for ROBOPuppet

 

Pocket Geometries
Our build process uses three pocket geometries that have been designed to ease the modification of all model links to fit the encoders and associated electronics and aid in assembly of the controller. These 3D models are subtracted from the mesh surface to provide structures for holding the base of the encoders in the joint surface and the the encoder shafts in the opposing joint surface.

Pocket Geometries include Joint Encoder Pockets and Joint Axis Pockets.

 

Joint Encoder Pocket - contains a bracket in which the joint potentiometer can be mounted and a fixture, a void, in which the base of the potentiometer resides

 

Joint Axis Pockets - contains the pocket into which the potentiometer shaft is affixed in order to ensure the motion of the controller part translates directly into motion in the potentiometer shaft. These pockets also allow space for bolts to be installed at the end of the potentiometer shaft giving control over the friction between the joint connected by the potentiometer.

 

Joint Encoder Assemblies - standardized, inexpensive assemblies that consist of brackets, encoders and bolts that are installed in the spaces provided by the pocket geometries and which enable the puppet to support its own weight against gravity.

 

Friction bolts - bolts added to the threaded end of the potentiometer shaft that allows for the control of friction between joint surfaces thus allowing for control of the action of the puppet.
